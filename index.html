Applause for the OpenPhoenux, please!

The GTA04, codename 'Phoenux', is the successor of 'Freerunner' (PDF), the first open mobile phone produced by OpenMoko, which dropped development in 2009. (See history for details.)

GoldenDelicious from Munich jumped in and decided to give the community new hope by equipping the beagleboard with all necessary features for a modern phone with

  • Full Linux support
  • Open schematics
  • Open layout
  • Expansion connectors
  • Lots of documentation
  • many components

What began as an upgrade board for the GTA02 became an independent phone when new cases were developed.

And there are more options:

  • Take the schematics and do what you want!
  • Equip it with a camera
  • Create a new case design
  • Test and develop the hardware keyboard
  • Have a look at the contribution page.
  • Be generous.
  • Be creative.

Phoenux Hardware Specifications

CPU & Graphic

  • Processor: DM3730 (OMAP3), 800 MHz, ARMv7 (Cortex A8), NEON, integrated DSP (TMS320C64x)
  • OpenGL© ES 2.0 capable 2D/3D graphics accelerator
  • 2.8 inch LCD with VGA (640x480) and touch screen (single touch with pressure detection)

Memory

  • 512 MB RAM
  • 512 MB NAND Flash
  • microSDHC (up to 32 GB)

Wireless

  • Quad-Band GSM, EDGE, UMTS HSDPA 14.4 MBit/s, HSUPA 5.76 MBit/s Specs
  • GPS 20 channel, int/ext. Antenna
  • FM receiver and transmitter, both with RDS
  • 802.11b/g, Bluetooth 2.0
  • RFID tag chip with 64 kBit EEPROM

Periphals

  • USB 2.0: full OTG (Client, Charger, Host)
  • Camera 1.3 Mpx (optional accessory)
  • Sensors: 3-axis Accelerometer, 3-axis Compass, 3-axis Gyroscope, Barometer/Thermometer Ambient Light
  • Audio / Video: Ear, Microphone, (Stereo) Handsfree, 2.5mm 4pin AV Jack (Composite-Video- Out, Audio in/out, Remote Control)
  • stereo speaker support (only for Neo1973/GTA01 case)
For a complete list with a diff to former revisions check this feature list.

Misc

  • Battery: replaceable 3.7 V LiIon, 1200 mAh
  • Others: 2 Buttons, 4 LEDs
  • internal RS232 (full level, RX/TX/RTS/CTS/RI) - for debugging, unbricking and optional console
  • Torch/Flash controller

Development

  • fits into existing Openmoko Neo1973 or Freerunner case (reversible)
  • 2x 60 pin expansion connector (optional)
  • documentation (see Manual incl. schematics) of all GPIO and signal connections to support driver development and extensions.

Price

We need your money!

Yes, we don't like to say, but the lights are red until we receive your support.

Society breaks apart in people who want to use things and others who want to personalize them. Let us know, what you want and we will find a way!

Diversity is great but think of the price. To minimize costs we need to raise the numbers as much as possible, to comply the needs of affordable community hardware.

Alas our price of above 500 € is still high due to the small batch size because there are not enough orders to start.

The next revision with 200 units could be produced within ca. 8-10 months, but we need 120.000 € first. Have a look at the graph below:

Is there no solution?

Our current strategy is twofold. While we try to raise funds we collect your orders within a preferred price range. With regular updates we inform the community about the current calculated price and everybody can decide to jump in when we reach an acceptable threshold.

Think of it as a train waiting for green lights as soon as enough passengers got in.

I want one. Now!

Ok, for developers there are some last units of the GTA04A4. They are not perfect, please ask goldendelicious for a detailed list.

We offer three different product options:

  • GTA04 motherboard for DIY upgrades.
  • Letux 2804: preinstalled in a refurbished Openmoko GTA02 case.
  • GTA04 Custom: contact us for a customized variant.
This is not a complete phone. You need to get a new or used Neo 1973 or Neo Freerunner unless you purchase one of the rare complete Letux 2804 units. Or you take a Case Kit plus a 3D printed or 3D cut case.

Key features are:

  • ARM Cortex A8 processor with 1000 MHz and 512 MB RAM
  • UMTS 3.75G (14 MBit/s max.)
  • Integrated sensors (Barometer, Gyroscope, Accelerometer, Compass)
  • optional: Camera
  • USB OTG 2.0
  • fits into existing Neo Freerunner case

Why?

What the inventors of PC and WWW did want

computation, storage and communication is personal
WWW connects decentralized servers and clients to share information
everyone controls his/her data published to the net

Phoenux stands for

Open Phone running Linux
participation by everyone
makes the technical system transparent, not the user
allows to inspect what the system is doing
open schematics and good documentation
extensible hard- and software
fights planned obsolescence through open hard- and software
long-term support (e.g. software upgrades for an 2007 Neo 1973)
Do It Yourself, eg.
community created cases
(wooden, 3D printed, aluminium)
can be repaired using standard parts
no central, intransparent, stock
hardware production near to users (Europe) under fair labour conditions
independent from the "modern mainframe" and back to the networked, decentralized web
everyone controls his/her data published to the net
you play client and server roles and keep control over your participation

Problems with closed phones

Why Android and iOS are not open enough.

They don't care about your privacy.
Try to secure your phone and data.
Try to understand why your device fails in a specific situation.
Try to get an upgrade for your 2 years old Android device.
not every bit and piece is open and extensible or even long-living
Try to provide your own home screen to iOS. Even Facebook Home failed.
Try to connect a hardware keyboard. If you are lucky you have an USB host port. But do you have the driver?

News

News & Updates

GTA04A5 is ready for production since summer 2012. There are few pieces left to be assembled in different cases (see pictures on the left).

The current mainboard revision is ready to go to production as soon as enough orders drop in. To enter next step of production we need at least 300 orders, best would be 100.000. Have a look at the price page for details.

We opened the preorder phase and send monthly announcements to all members of our wishlist. For detailed updates you can subcribe the mailing list.

"If 200 people agree to put 599 EUR on the table we could start production tomorrow (well, we need 6-8 weeks to get the components) and 200 GTA04 boards would be available in November. We could also order 200 3D-printed cases, earpieces etc. to make complete phones. One issue is that we have just ~70 display modules, and they are out of production for a long time. But there may be some remaining stock in Asia, so it appears to be a solvable problem. Doing a redesign for a different display, different case (N900) is also possible, but takes more time (estimate 4-6 months). But it also needs 200 * 599 EUR on the table. Not to start design or production (that has become my hobby), but to buy components." (Dr. H. Nikolaus Schaller, August 23th 2013)

Major improvements of this revision:

  • added DIY antenna
  • 3D printed case for daily use
  • make it an independ device (without the need to disassemble a GTA02)
  • Small modifications to replace obsolete components or improve production process

About

Who we are

This is an interesting question as there is a heterogenous global community fascinated by the idea of open mobile phones and open hardware in general.

Many of "us" care a lot about private communcation - in case you are working for some intelligence agency better bookmark this page.

Check out the community portal in the wiki. It's a bit outdated but a great resource.

We long for

  • completely open phone (100% software, 99% hardware)
  • privacy enhancements
  • fair labour conditions for production and assembly
  • clean resources (beyond war areas / human rights violations)
  • open process - everybody can join in and contribute

For more information see Facts and Reasons and the Wikipedia article for the complete picture.
